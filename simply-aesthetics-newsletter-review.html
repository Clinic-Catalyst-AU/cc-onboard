Your skin noticed before you did

Week one · the honest explanation

Hi Sarah,

There is usually a moment when you realise your skin is not behaving quite the way it used to.

Think of collagen as the framework sitting underneath your skin, a bit like the springs in a good mattress. For years they hold their shape without you giving them a second thought. Then gradually they give a little, and one day the whole thing sits differently.

Collagen is the protein that gives skin its structure and its bounce. We all start making less of it as we get older, slowly at first, and then it picks up pace around perimenopause when oestrogen begins to drop away. Oestrogen also helps skin hold onto moisture, which is why dryness and dullness so often turn up at the same time as everything else.

That is usually the real reason makeup starts to sit in lines that were not bothering you a year ago.

None of this means something has gone wrong. It means your skin is asking for a bit more support than it used to need, and that is a very normal thing to happen.

Everyone's skin is different, and what helps yours will not be what helps someone else's. That is the whole reason we start with a proper assessment and one honest conversation, rather than jumping straight to a treatment.

I get asked sometimes why a first appointment with me takes as long as it does, when nothing much appears to be happening.

What actually happens when you come in

Week three · no surprises

Hi Sarah,

If you have not been into a skin clinic before, not knowing what happens can be the very thing that keeps you putting it off. So here is the whole appointment, start to finish.

We talk first. You tell me what you have noticed and what is bothering you. I ask about your health, any medications, and what your skin has had done before, because all of it matters.

Then I look properly. A close look at your skin in good light, so we are both working from what is actually there rather than what either of us assumes.

We talk through the options. What might suit, what would not, and what I would genuinely leave alone for now. If something is not right for you, I will say so.

You decide in your own time. You leave with a plan, and written aftercare if we do go ahead. If you would rather go home and think about it, that is completely fine.

I am a registered nurse and I have a special interest in natural results, which in practice usually means I am the one suggesting we do less rather than more.

Everyone's skin is different, so I will not tell you what your results will look like. What I will tell you is that you will understand exactly what we are doing and why, before anything begins.
