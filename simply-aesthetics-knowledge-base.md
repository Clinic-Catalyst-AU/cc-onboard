## Set 2 - keyword-researched (Semrush AU search data)

**Q: What is collagen and what does it actually do?**
A: Collagen is the protein that gives skin its structure and bounce. We make less of it as
we age, and that drop speeds up around perimenopause, which is often when women notice
their skin looking less firm and makeup sitting differently. It's usually the thing behind
the change you're seeing.

**Q: Do collagen supplements and powders work?**
A: Honestly, the evidence is mixed, and they're not a substitute for looking after your
skin directly. What we focus on in clinic is encouraging your skin to build its own
collagen. If you're already taking one and enjoying it, there's no harm, just don't rely on
it alone.

**Q: What is skin needling?**
A: Skin needling uses very fine needles to create tiny, controlled channels in the skin,
which encourages your skin's own repair process and its own collagen and elastin. We use
SkinPen. It's one of the gentler places to start.

**Q: Does skin needling hurt?**
A: You do feel it, and most women describe it as very manageable. Julia talks you through
comfort before anything starts and checks in with you the whole way. You're in good hands,
and nothing begins until you're ready.

**Q: How many sessions will I need?**
A: It depends on your skin and what you're hoping to see, so Julia maps that out with you
at your consultation. It's usually planned as a course rather than a one-off, and you'll
know the plan and the cost before you commit to anything.

**Q: What's the downtime, and when can I wear makeup or wash my face?**
A: You can expect some redness for a day or two, a bit like mild sunburn. Makeup and
washing are usually paused for a short window afterwards while your skin settles, and Julia
gives you written aftercare on the day so you're never left guessing.

**Q: Can I have skin needling while pregnant or breastfeeding?**
A: This is one to talk through properly rather than answer over chat, because it depends on
your circumstances. Let us know when you get in touch and Julia will go through your
options with you at consultation.

**Q: Does skin needling help with fine lines?**
A: Fine lines and skin texture are among the concerns it targets, by supporting your skin's
own collagen. What that looks like differs for everyone, which is why Julia looks at your
skin properly first rather than promising you anything upfront.

**Q: Does menopause affect your skin?**
A: It really does. Falling oestrogen means less collagen and less moisture held in the
skin, so a lot of women notice dryness, dullness and makeup catching in lines. If that's
what's happening for you, you're not imagining it, and it's very common.

**Q: What is perimenopause, and when does it start showing in your skin?**
A: Perimenopause is the stretch of years leading up to menopause, and it often begins in
the forties. Skin changes are one of the earlier signs women notice. For anything medical
about perimenopause itself, your GP is the right person. We're here for the skin side of it.
