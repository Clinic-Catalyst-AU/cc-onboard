Clinic Catalyst

Your system, on two computers

Clinic: your clinic. One Business Brain, one set of skills, both machines always in step.

The idea in one line. Your skills get installed separately on each computer, and your Business Brain lives in Dropbox so both computers read and write the same one. Change something on the laptop, it is already there on the desktop.

Computer 1 - your main machine

Do this first, all the way through
1

Install the system

Open Terminal and paste this. It takes 25 to 45 minutes, mostly downloading. You can keep working while it runs.

curl -fsSL https://clinic-catalyst-au.github.io/cc-onboard/clinic-bootstrap.sh | bash

This installs Claude Code, the tools it needs, all your Clinic Catalyst skills, and creates your ~/Clinic folder.

2

Sign in to Claude

Close Terminal, open a fresh one, and type claude. Sign in with your own Claude account when it asks.

3

Move your Business Brain into Dropbox

This is the step that makes two computers possible. Paste this in Terminal, one line at a time.

mv ~/Clinic ~/Dropbox/Clinic ln -s ~/Dropbox/Clinic ~/Clinic

Your Brain now physically lives in Dropbox, but everything still finds it at ~/Clinic exactly as before. Nothing you type changes.

4

Wait for Dropbox to finish syncing

Check the Dropbox icon in your menu bar shows a green tick before you touch the second computer. This matters.

Computer 2 - your other machine

Only after computer 1 has fully synced
1

Install Dropbox and let it sync

Sign in to the same Dropbox account. Wait until you can see the Clinic folder with your files actually in it.

2

Install the system

Same command as before. This machine needs its own copy of Claude Code and the skills.

curl -fsSL https://clinic-catalyst-au.github.io/cc-onboard/clinic-bootstrap.sh | bash
3

Point this machine at the shared Brain

The install made a fresh empty ~/Clinic here. Replace it with a link to the Dropbox one.

rm -rf ~/Clinic ln -s ~/Dropbox/Clinic ~/Clinic
Check before you run this. Only delete ~/Clinic on the second computer, and only if it is the empty one the installer just made. If you have real work in it, move that into Dropbox first instead.
4

Sign in to Claude and test it

Open Terminal, type claude, sign in, then ask it something about your clinic. If it knows your offers and your voice, both machines are sharing one Brain and you are done.

Keeping both machines current

When new skills land

Run this on each computer. It only refreshes the skills and never touches your Business Brain or your own work.

curl -fsSL https://clinic-catalyst-au.github.io/cc-onboard/clinic-update.sh | bash

Then close and reopen Claude Code so the new skills register.

Windows machine? Use these instead:

irm https://clinic-catalyst-au.github.io/cc-onboard/clinic-bootstrap.ps1 | iex irm https://clinic-catalyst-au.github.io/cc-onboard/clinic-update.ps1 | iex

Good habits with two computers

Do not run Claude on both machines at the same moment on the same files. Dropbox is quick but it is not instant, and two Claudes writing at once is how you end up with a conflicted copy.

Let Dropbox settle before you switch machines. Green tick, then move.

If you ever see a "conflicted copy" file, open both, keep the newer one, delete the other. Nothing is lost, it just means both machines wrote at once.

Update the skills on both machines on the same day. Otherwise one computer has commands the other does not.
