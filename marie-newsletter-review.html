Why your skin feels tight by Thursday

Hi Sarah,

Winter does something specific to skin, and it is worth understanding because it changes what your skin actually needs right now.

Think of your skin barrier as the mortar between bricks. It holds water in and keeps irritation out. Cold air outside, heating inside, and hotter showers than usual all pull moisture out of that mortar faster than your skin can replace it.

So the barrier thins. And a thin barrier does not just feel tight and look dull. It often reacts by producing more oil to compensate, which is why some people break out more in winter, not less. Congestion in July surprises a lot of our clients.

An example of the congestion we work with at the clinic. Shared with permission. Every skin responds differently, and what suits one person will not suit another.

The instinct is to strip it back with something stronger. Usually the opposite is what helps. Support the barrier first, then treat. Less is more, and in winter that is not a slogan, it is the whole approach.

Three things that genuinely help this month: shorten your showers, add a barrier support step rather than swapping your whole routine, and keep wearing SPF every day. Yes, even in July. UV does not take winter off.

What actually happens in your first visit

Hi Sarah,

People are often a bit nervous before a first skin appointment, usually because they do not know what they are walking into or worry they will be talked into something. So here is exactly how it goes.

We sit down first. We go through your history, what you have tried, what your skin does across a normal week, and what is actually bothering you. Not what we think should bother you.

Then we look properly. Clinical photography, a skin analysis and a barrier test, so we are working from what is in front of us rather than a guess. You see the photos too. Most people have never seen their own skin at that magnification and it explains a lot.

Only then do we talk about what to do, in what order, and over what timeframe. Sometimes the answer is a treatment. Quite often the first answer is homecare and SPF, because there is no point resurfacing skin that is still struggling to hold water.

Skin we have worked with at the clinic, shared with permission. This is an example of the concerns we see, not a prediction. Your skin, your history and your plan are yours alone.

Five years in, that order has not changed. Assess, explain, then treat. It is slower to sell and much better for your skin.
