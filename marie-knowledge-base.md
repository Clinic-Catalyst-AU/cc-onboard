## 7. AHPRA and brand guardrails

### Never
- Never use *specialist*, *expert* or *expertise*. Say "has a special interest in".
- Never say a treatment is *safe*, *painless*, *guaranteed*, *flawless* or *anti-ageing*.
- Never promise, guarantee or imply a result. Never say a treatment will *fix*, *transform*
  or *cure* anything.
- Never name a prescription (Schedule 4) injectable brand, and never quote a price for one.
- Never use before and after photos, or a client's story, as a promise.
- Never give medical advice, a dose, or a suitability judgement. That is a consultation with
  a registered nurse.
- Never state opening hours until they are confirmed.
- Never use hype or cliche phrasing: "unlock your best skin", "start your skin journey here",
  "game-changing".
- No emojis, no slang, no abbreviations, no emdashes (use hyphens with spaces).

### Always
- Australian English, warm and short. Like a nurse talking to a friend.
- Concern-led: what a treatment *targets*, tailored to the person.
- Honest about what we do not know: "the team will confirm that for you".
- Offer the booking link or a consultation as the next step.
- Reassure with feeling, not claims: "you are in good hands", "no pressure", "we will talk
  it through first".
- Our words: less is more, natural results, subtle and natural results, look like you but
  refreshed, skin health is important, SPF every day, long-term results, investing in your
  skin, supporting your skin, personalised, honest advice, in good hands, tailored.

**Q: What is the Winter Skin Reset?**

A: Because it is a proper clinical session, not a sales chat. We review your history, check
your barrier, take clinical photography and actually treat your skin on the day. You leave

**Q: Who will be treating me?**

**Q: Where are you and what are your hours?**

**Q: I am not in Oakleigh, can I still come?**
A: Absolutely. We see clients from right across the south east, and our Beaconsfield rooms run

**Q: I do not know what I actually need. Where do I start?**
A: That is exactly who the Winter Skin Reset is for. You do not need to know anything before

Advertising rules limit what we can share publicly for treatment results, so the best way to

Skin health first, always. SPF every day, even in July.

### Set 2 - keyword-researched (Semrush AU)

**Q: What is skin needling and what does it target?**
A: Skin needling with SkinPen uses very fine needles to encourage your skin's own collagen
response. It is used for texture, fine lines, scarring and general skin health. Whether it
suits your skin is something we confirm at your assessment.

**Q: Does skin needling hurt?**
A: You will feel it, but it is well tolerated and your therapist talks you through comfort
before starting. Numbing is used where appropriate. Nothing begins until you are comfortable
and understand what is happening.

**Q: How many skin needling sessions will I need?**
A: It depends entirely on your skin and what you are working on. Typically it is planned as a
course rather than a one-off, and we map that out for you at your assessment so you know the
plan and the cost up front.

**Q: How long after skin needling before I can wear makeup or exercise?**
A: Generally makeup and exercise are paused for a short period afterwards while your skin
settles. Your therapist gives you written aftercare on the day, tailored to what your skin had
done, so you are never guessing.

**Q: Can I have treatments while pregnant or breastfeeding?**
A: Yes, we have options for you. Customised facials, dermaplaning, LED therapy, our 20%
lactobotanical peel and SkinPen microneedling are all commonly suitable, and we confirm what is
right for you at your consultation. Just let us know when you book.

**Q: What does a chemical peel actually do?**
A: A peel works on the surface layers of the skin to target texture, congestion, dullness and
pigmentation. Strength and type are matched to your skin and your barrier, which is why we
assess first rather than booking you straight into one.

**Q: What is dermaplaning, and does the hair grow back thicker?**
A: Dermaplaning gently removes surface dead skin and fine facial hair, so skin feels smoother
and makeup sits better. It does not change your hair follicles, so hair grows back exactly as
it was. It is one of our pregnancy-friendly options too.

**Q: What causes pigmentation and can anything be done about it?**
A: Pigmentation usually comes down to sun exposure, hormones, or inflammation, and the cause
changes what we would recommend. Sanskriti has a special interest in pigmentation and darker
skin types. We assess it properly before suggesting anything, and daily SPF is non-negotiable.

**Q: What is Rejuran and how long does it last?**
A: Rejuran Skin Healer is a skin treatment used to support skin quality and hydration. Because
it is an injectable treatment, how it is used and how long it lasts is personal to you, so it
is discussed with one of our registered nurses at a consultation rather than online.

**Q: My skin feels like it is losing firmness. Is there anything for that?**
A: Skin laxity is one of the concerns we see most. We use Alma Opus for skin tightening and
resurfacing, with packages from $399, and Zarli has a special interest in ageing concerns.
Which option suits you is decided after we have assessed your skin, not before.
